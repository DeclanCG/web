Conclusion

The approach I had taken when creating my character was the period of time he was from. I already knew what I wanted to create as I play alot of shooting games (Call of Duty) and wanted to create a soldier in war time. I had to think if he was from present, future or past and originally thought of using the past but I was warned that this kind of character has been created over and over again as a WW Soldier. So I had to improvise to create the solider futureistic. After I had created the kind of character I wanted to create I had started on the background as he would of been in a futuristic city with some old buildings standing from 2010's (The time is set around 2040 were technolgy has advanced). When going through colour design I had picked 4 different colours 2 primary and 2 secondary, the primary being Blue and Green while the second colours being mustard yellow and red. I played around with different tones of the primary colours as to mix and match the flavours of the colour. I had some feedback given to me saying the yellow and green looked a bit like the Mandolorian from Star wars so I tried adapting both colour palates together to create something completly different and unqie. Having limted skill I had a small bit of help with creating rough sketches as I was given a few lines to follow I had went off my own path away from block building to lines roughing around everywhere to find a different view on the character. Eventuraly I found the rough sketches to complex to replecate into a final form. So I dumbed it down to a simplier version and had used him in my final piece.
